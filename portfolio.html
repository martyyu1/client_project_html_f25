Portfolio

Welcome to Portfolio Development

Your professional portfolio is a powerful tool to showcase your skills, experiences, and potential to employers, clients, or academic programs. Whether you're building your very first website or refining a design case study, this page will guide you through every step.

Explore essential resources, get inspired by peer examples, and learn how to tell your story effectively. Use the quick links below to jump directly to your area of interest, and check out the FAQs and "Most Popular Resources" to get started right away!

Building a Strong Portfolio: Key Steps

  1. Select Your Best Work: Choose projects, papers, presentations, and other work that highlight your skills and achievements.
  2. Organize by Theme or Skill: Group your work in a way that tells a coherent story about your capabilities and growth.
  3. Add Context: For each item, include a brief description of the project, your role, the skills you utilized, and the outcomes.
  4. Use a Professional Format: Whether digital or physical, ensure your portfolio is well-designed, easy to navigate, and free of errors.
  5. Keep It Updated: Regularly add new work and remove outdated items to keep your portfolio current and relevant.

FAQs

  • Do I really need a portfolio?
    For most UMSI tracks and roles in information, a professional online portfolio is strongly recommendedâ€”even required for some programs or employers. It's the best way to showcase what you can do, in your own words and style.
  • What should I include in my portfolio?
    Focus on high-quality work samples (class projects, internships, volunteer work), your process, and brief reflections on what you learned or accomplished.
  • Should I create a digital or physical portfolio?
    A digital portfolio is often more versatile and accessible, but consider your audience and the context in which you'll be presenting it.
  • How often should I update my portfolio?
    Regularly update your portfolio to include new work and remove outdated items, ideally every few months or after completing significant projects.
  • Can someone review my portfolio before I apply to jobs?
    Absolutely. Schedule a portfolio review with a Career Development Office (CDO) coach for in-depth feedback.
